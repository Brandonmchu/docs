## Overview

Invoices are the core of Every AI. Create professional, branded invoices, send them to clients, track delivery and opens, accept payments, and manage your entire billing lifecycle.

## Invoice Workflow States

Every invoice moves through defined states during its lifecycle:

### Draft
- Work in progress
- Not sent to client
- Can be edited freely
- Not visible to client
- No email sent

### Issued
- Sent to client
- Client has access
- Payment pending
- Email delivered
- Public page available

### Paid
- Payment received (full amount)
- No outstanding balance
- Payment recorded
- Status automatically updated
- Client notified (if online payment)

### Overdue
- Past due date
- No payment received
- Payment reminder recommended
- Status shown in red
- Tracked separately for follow-up

### Void
- Cancelled invoice
- No payment expected
- Removed from outstanding balance
- Preserved for records
- Cannot be un-voided

<Note>
  Invoice status updates automatically based on due dates and payments received.
</Note>

## Creating Invoices

### Basic Invoice Creation

1. Navigate to **Invoices** in main menu
2. Click **Create Invoice** or **+** button
3. Select a **client** (required)
4. Add **line items** (services/products)
5. Set **payment terms** (dates, reference)
6. Add optional **notes** and **attachments**
7. **Save as Draft** or **Send Invoice**

### Invoice Components

#### Client Selection

**Required**: Every invoice must have a client.

- Select from existing clients
- Client info appears on invoice:
  - Name
  - Email (invoice delivery)
  - Address
  - CC emails (if set up)
- Can't change client after invoice is issued

<Tip>
  If you need to invoice someone new, add them as a client first!
</Tip>

#### Line Items

Line items are the individual services or products you're billing.

**Line Item Fields:**
- **Description**: What you're billing for
- **Quantity**: How many units
- **Rate**: Price per unit
- **Tax**: Tax percentage (if applicable)
- **Total**: Calculated automatically (quantity × rate + tax)

**Adding Line Items:**

**Option 1: From Offerings** (Recommended)
1. Click **Add Line Item**
2. Select **Choose Offering**
3. Pick from your offering list
4. Details auto-fill (description, rate, tax)
5. Adjust quantity if needed

**Option 2: Manual Entry**
1. Click **Add Line Item**
2. Type description manually
3. Enter quantity and rate
4. Set tax rate
5. Save line item

**Option 3: From Time Records**
1. Select **Add from Time Tracking**
2. Choose unbilled time records
3. Time converts to line items
4. Calculation: hours × hourly rate
5. Time records marked as "billed"

**Option 4: From Expenses**
1. Select **Add from Expenses**
2. Choose billable expenses for this client
3. Expenses become line items
4. Includes expense amount and description
5. Expenses marked as "billed"

#### Financial Details

**Subtotal**: Sum of all line item amounts (before tax)

**Tax**: Applied per line item or to subtotal
- Set per line item, or
- Apply to entire invoice
- Automatic calculation
- Configurable tax rate

**Discounts** (Optional):
- **Percentage Discount**: 10% off, 15% off, etc.
- **Fixed Amount Discount**: $50 off, $100 off, etc.
- Applied to subtotal before tax
- Discount reason can be added

**Total Amount**:
```
Subtotal
- Discount
+ Tax
= Total Amount Due
```

Calculated automatically. No manual calculation needed!

#### Payment Terms

**Issue Date**: Date invoice is created/sent
- Defaults to today
- Can be backdated if needed

**Due Date**: Payment deadline
- Automatically calculated from organization default due days
- Can be set manually
- Determines when invoice becomes overdue

**Reference/PO Number** (Optional):
- Client's purchase order number
- Project reference
- Internal tracking code
- Helps client match to their records

**Online Payments**:
- Enable/disable per invoice
- Requires Stripe connection
- Adds "Pay Now" button to invoice
- Clients pay by credit card

<Tip>
  Set realistic due dates. Net 30 (30 days) is industry standard for most services.
</Tip>

#### Notes & Terms

Add custom notes that appear on the invoice:

**Common Uses:**
- Payment instructions
- Thank you message
- Specific terms for this project
- Late payment policies
- Bank transfer details

**Default Notes**:
- Organization default notes auto-fill
- Can be edited per invoice
- Useful for standard terms

**Example Note:**
```
Thank you for your business! Payment is due within 30 days.
Late payments subject to 1.5% monthly interest. For questions,
contact accounting@yourbusiness.com.
```

#### Attachments

Upload supporting documents to invoices:

**Supported Files:**
- PDFs (contracts, agreements)
- Images (receipts, photos)
- Word documents
- Excel spreadsheets
- Up to 5MB per file

**Attachment Features:**
- Visible to clients on public invoice page
- Downloadable by client
- Stored securely
- Multiple attachments per invoice

**Common Attachments:**
- Service contracts
- Work samples
- Receipt documentation
- Terms and conditions
- Detailed specifications

### Currency Selection

Choose currency per invoice:

**Supported:**
- USD (United States Dollar)
- CAD (Canadian Dollar)

**How it Works:**
- Organization default currency pre-selected
- Override per invoice if needed
- All amounts displayed in selected currency
- Automatic formatting ($ symbol)

**Use Cases:**
- US business billing Canadian client (CAD)
- Canadian business billing US client (USD)
- Mixed client base in both countries

<Note>
  Currency cannot be changed after invoice is issued.
</Note>

### Templates & Branding

Invoice templates control visual appearance:

**Template Features:**
- Organization logo
- Brand colors
- Professional layouts
- Consistent styling

**Templates Available:**
- Modern
- Classic
- Professional
- Creative

**Template Selection:**
- Default: Organization's selected template
- Override: Choose different template per invoice
- Preview before sending

**What's Branded:**
- Invoice header with logo
- Color accents
- Typography

All branding from organization settings applied automatically!

### Scheduled Sends

Queue invoices to send automatically on future date:

**How Scheduled Sends Work:**
1. Create invoice
2. Instead of "Send Now", choose **Schedule Send**
3. Select future date and time
4. Invoice saved as draft with scheduled task
5. System automatically sends on scheduled date
6. Email delivered, status changes to Issued

**Use Cases:**
- Schedule month-end invoicing
- Send on project completion date
- Coordinate with client's billing cycle
- Queue multiple invoices to send together

**Managing Scheduled Sends:**
- View scheduled date on invoice
- Edit schedule before send time
- Cancel schedule if needed
- Invoice remains draft until send

<Tip>
  Scheduled sends are editable until the send time, so you can make changes if needed!
</Tip>

## Sending Invoices

### Sending Process

**To Send an Invoice:**

1. Review invoice details
2. Check client email (and CC emails)
3. Preview invoice appearance
4. Click **Send Invoice**
5. Confirm send

**What Happens:**
- Email sent to client (and CC recipients)
- Invoice status changes to **Issued**
- Public invoice page created
- Email tracking begins
- Client can view and pay invoice

**Email Contains:**
- Invoice PDF attachment
- Link to public invoice page
- Payment instructions
- Professional branded message

### Email Delivery Tracking

Track every step of invoice delivery:

**Tracked Events:**
- **Delivered**: Email successfully delivered
- **Opened**: Client opened the email
- **Clicked**: Client clicked invoice link
- **Bounced**: Email failed to deliver
- **Spam**: Marked as spam (rare)

**View Tracking:**
1. Open invoice detail page
2. See **Email Activity** section
3. View all events with timestamps

**Benefits:**
- Know when client saw invoice
- Time follow-ups appropriately
- Identify delivery issues
- Measure engagement

<Tip>
  Wait to follow up until you see the client opened the email. No need to bother them before they've seen it!
</Tip>

### Resending Invoices

Need to send again? Easy:

1. Open invoice
2. Click **Resend Invoice**
3. Optionally add a message
4. Click **Send**

**Resend Tracking:**
- New email event created
- Separate tracking for resend
- Full history preserved

**When to Resend:**
- Client didn't receive original
- Client lost invoice
- Need to remind about payment
- Update client on payment terms

## Public Invoice Pages

Every issued invoice has a secure, branded public page.

### Public Page Features

**Client View:**
- Full invoice details
- Organization branding (logo, colors)
- Line items and totals
- Payment terms
- Attachments (downloadable)
- Pay Now button (if Stripe connected)
- Mobile-responsive design

**Security:**
- Unique, non-guessable URL
- No login required
- Secure HTTPS
- Optional password protection (future)

**Professional Appearance:**
- Branded with your organization
- Clean, modern design
- Print-friendly
- PDF download option

### Payment on Public Page

If Stripe is connected:

1. Client opens public invoice page
2. Clicks **Pay Now** button
3. Enters credit card details
4. Payment processes through Stripe
5. Invoice marked as **Paid** automatically
6. Client and business receive confirmation

Clients can pay invoices anytime, 24/7, from anywhere!

## Managing Invoices

### Invoice List View

**Features:**
- **Summary Cards**: Quick stats (total, paid, overdue)
- **Filters**: Status, client, date range
- **Search**: By invoice number, client, amount
- **Sort**: Date, amount, status, client
- **Bulk Actions**: Send, archive, delete multiple invoices

**Status Filters:**
- All Invoices
- Drafts
- Issued (unpaid)
- Paid
- Overdue
- Void

### Editing Invoices

**Draft Invoices:**
- Edit freely
- Change any field
- Add/remove line items
- Update client (before issuing)

**Issued Invoices:**
- Limited editing
- Can't change amounts or client
- Can add attachments
- Can add notes
- Can record payments

<Warning>
  Once an invoice is issued, major changes are restricted. Create a new invoice or void the old one if significant changes are needed.
</Warning>

### Voiding Invoices

Cancel invoices that won't be paid:

**To Void:**
1. Open invoice
2. Click **Void Invoice**
3. Add reason (optional)
4. Confirm voiding

**What Happens:**
- Status changes to **Void**
- Removed from outstanding balances
- Hidden from overdue list
- Preserved in history
- Cannot be un-voided

**When to Void:**
- Client cancelled project
- Invoice created in error
- Service not provided
- Replaced by new invoice

**Alternative to Voiding:**
- Apply full discount
- Record $0 payment
- Mark as paid without payment

### Duplicating Invoices

Create copy of existing invoice:

1. Open invoice
2. Click **Duplicate Invoice**
3. New draft created with same:
   - Client
   - Line items
   - Notes
   - Terms
4. Adjust as needed
5. Send new invoice

**Use Cases:**
- Recurring manual invoicing
- Similar projects for same client
- Template for similar services
- Correcting issued invoice (void original, send duplicate with changes)

### Archiving Invoices

Archive old paid invoices:

**To Archive:**
1. Select invoices
2. Click **Archive** bulk action
3. Invoices hidden from main view

**Archived Invoices:**
- Hidden from default list
- Still accessible via filter
- Included in reports
- Can be restored
- Data preserved

**When to Archive:**
- Old paid invoices
- Completed projects
- Clean up invoice list
- End of fiscal year

## Common Workflows

### Standard Invoicing Workflow

1. Create invoice
2. Select client
3. Add line items from offerings
4. Review totals
5. Add notes if needed
6. Attach contract (if applicable)
7. Send invoice
8. Track email delivery and opens
9. Client pays online or sends payment
10. Record payment (or automatic if Stripe)
11. Invoice marked as paid

### Time-Based Invoicing

1. Track time during work period
2. Create invoice at billing cycle
3. Select client

5. Review hours and rates
6. Send invoice
7. Time records marked as billed

1. Log expenses throughout project
2. Mark expenses as billable to client

4. Add billable expenses as line items
5. Include other services
6. Send invoice
7. Expenses marked as billed

## Troubleshooting

### Invoice Not Sending

**Check:**
- Client has valid email address
- Internet connection active
- Email service operational
- Client email not typo

**Solution:**
- Verify client email
- Try resending
- Check email delivery status
- Contact support if persistent

### Client Says They Didn't Receive Invoice

**Steps:**
1. Check email delivery status
2. Verify email address correct
3. Ask client to check spam folder
4. Resend invoice
5. Send public invoice link directly
6. Try CC email if available

### Can't Edit Issued Invoice

**Reason:** Protection against altering invoices after sending

**Solutions:**
- Void invoice and create new one
- Add credit note (future feature)
- Record payment adjustment
- Contact client to explain change

### Wrong Total Calculation

**Check:**
- Tax applied correctly per line item
- Discount applied correctly
- All line items included
- Currency correct
- No duplicate line items

**Solution:**
- Review each line item
- Recalculate manually to verify
- Check tax settings
- Contact support if calculation appears wrong
